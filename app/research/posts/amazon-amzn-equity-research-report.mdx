## Investment Thesis (Summary)

Amazon is a scaled flywheel across e-commerce, third-party marketplace, advertising, and cloud computing (AWS). AWS remains the profit engine and a durable platform for the adoption of AI workloads, while the retail network—now more regionalized—continues to improve delivery speed and unit cost. Advertising expands high-margin monetization across retail surfaces and streaming properties. We see a Wide moat supported by network effects, cost advantages, and switching costs (Prime, seller services, AWS ecosystems). Uncertainty remains elevated given macro sensitivity in retail, competitive pricing dynamics, regulatory scrutiny, and capital intensity in infrastructure.

## Business Strategy & Outlook

**Retail flywheel.** Amazon’s first- and third-party retail model benefits from scale efficiencies, dense last-mile, and selection breadth that reinforce consumer and seller network effects. The continued shift to same-day/next-day delivery via regionalized fulfillment lowers unit cost and raises conversion.

**AWS platform.** AWS remains a leader in cloud infrastructure with an expanding AI stack (Bedrock, Trainium/Inferentia, managed model services) positioned to capture generative AI training and inference. We expect steady growth as enterprises modernize workloads and adopt AI-native apps.

**Advertising.** Retail media and streaming ads are structurally high-margin and under-monetized relative to engagement. Ad load optimization and commerce integration support multi-year growth.

**Devices and services.** Amazon continues to iterate on devices and content. In FY2024, the company launched a new lineup of Kindle devices, including the first color Kindle and a revamped Kindle Scribe, expanding the e-reader franchise.

---

## Economic Moat — **Wide**

* **Network Effects (Marketplace & Prime):** More sellers drive selection and lower prices, attracting more buyers; Prime loyalty deepens engagement with fast shipping and bundled services.
* **Cost Advantage (Logistics & Scale):** Distribution density, procurement leverage, and proprietary software yield lower unit economics that are hard to replicate.
* **Switching Costs (AWS):** Application architectures, data gravity, and integrated services (databases, analytics, AI) create switching frictions that compound over time.

## Fair Value Estimate — **$205 per share** (Base Case)

We employ a 10-year, three-stage DCF:

* **Revenue path (illustrative):** Mid-teens growth near term (AWS + Ads outpacing retail), decelerating to high single digits at scale.
* **FCF margins:** Consolidated mid-teens long-run, reflecting AWS mix and retail efficiency offsets.
* **WACC:** **8.5%**; **Terminal growth:** **4.0%**.

At today’s price, the market discounts either higher mid-cycle AWS growth, faster ad monetization, or a lower cost of capital than our base. A reverse-DCF suggests the current price implies moderately stronger decade-end FCF or a modestly lower WACC relative to our inputs.

## Risk & Uncertainty — **High**

* **Regulation & Antitrust:** Ongoing scrutiny of marketplace practices, self-preferencing, and labor standards can raise costs or constrain strategy.
* **Competition & Pricing:** Aggressive competition in e-commerce and cloud can pressure take-rates and margins; hyperscaler price moves ripple into AWS.
* **Macro Sensitivity:** Consumer demand and FX volatility affect retail; capex cycles and IT budgets influence AWS trajectories.
* **Execution & Capital Intensity:** Large, multi-year logistics and data center investments carry utilization and return risks.

## Bulls Say / Bears Say

**Bulls Say**

1. AWS scale and AI services provide a durable growth and margin engine.
2. Retail media (Ads) is an under-monetized, high-margin profit stream.
3. Regionalized fulfillment lowers cost-to-serve and widens the moat.

**Bears Say**

1. Regulatory constraints could dilute marketplace economics and strategic agility.
2. Cloud pricing pressure and competitive parity slow AWS margin expansion.
3. Retail margins remain structurally thin, limiting consolidated FCF.
