Microsoft has made a striking strategic pivot in Visual Studio Code, introducing an automatic AI model selection feature that **defaults to Anthropic's Claude Sonnet 4 over OpenAI's GPT-5** for coding tasks. The feature, rolled out September 15, signals a broader realignment in Microsoft’s AI approach—despite its $13 billion investment in OpenAI since 2019.

## Internal benchmarks drive preference

According to Microsoft’s announcement, the new auto-selection system rotates models for free-tier GitHub Copilot users, but **paid subscribers will “primarily rely on Claude Sonnet 4.”**  

The decision reflects months of internal testing. Julia Liuson, president of Microsoft’s developer division, noted in a June internal email that Claude Sonnet 4 was recommended based on benchmark results—a position unchanged even after GPT-5’s release.

GitHub engineers reported that Claude Sonnet 4 “stays on track longer, understands problems more deeply, and provides more elegant code.” On SWE-bench, a key software engineering benchmark, Claude Sonnet 4 reached 72–73% accuracy, outperforming GPT-5 and other rivals in subtle but important workflows.

## Expanding beyond VS Code

Microsoft is also **testing Anthropic models inside Microsoft 365 Copilot**, particularly in Excel automation and PowerPoint generation.  

This arrangement is unusual: to access Claude, Microsoft pays Amazon Web Services—even though AWS competes with Azure. Despite higher costs, Microsoft plans to keep Copilot’s $30/month pricing unchanged.

## Partnership restructuring with OpenAI

The shift comes as Microsoft and OpenAI renegotiate their long-standing partnership. On September 11, the two signed a **non-binding memorandum of understanding** to restructure revenue sharing. OpenAI’s share to Microsoft is set to decline from 20% to roughly 8–10% by 2030, potentially freeing up **$50B+ in extra revenue** for OpenAI as it pursues public benefit corporation status.

Meanwhile, Microsoft AI CEO Mustafa Suleyman revealed that its **MAI-1-preview** model was trained on 15,000 Nvidia H100 chips—“tiny” compared to future clusters expected to be six to ten times larger.

## Multi-model strategy takes shape

Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella underscored the **multi-model direction**, saying the company will support “a variety of models” across products. For developers, Copilot’s auto-selection means:

- 10% request discount for paid users  
- Reduced rate limiting  
- Transparent model attribution (users can hover to see which model answered)  

The decision highlights a growing reality in enterprise AI: performance benchmarks and practical outcomes increasingly drive adoption, not just vendor alliances. By prioritizing Claude Sonnet 4 in VS Code while maintaining deep ties with OpenAI, Microsoft signals a **future of mixed-model ecosystems** in productivity and developer tools.
