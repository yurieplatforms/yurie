Apple announced at its **"Awe Dropping"** event today that iOS 26 and visionOS 26 will launch on **Monday, September 15**, alongside the company’s full suite of updated operating systems. The release marks one of the most comprehensive software updates in Apple’s history, introducing the revolutionary **Liquid Glass** design language across all platforms.

![Apple iOS 26 Liquid Glass](/PPLX6-IMG.jpg)

## Major design overhaul with Liquid Glass

The September 15 release will bring Apple’s first universal design language across all operating systems. **Liquid Glass**, inspired by the translucent interface of visionOS, features glass-like transparency and fluidity that dynamically adapts to content and lighting conditions. It represents Apple’s most significant visual refresh since **iOS 7 in 2013**.

According to Alan Dye, Apple’s VP of Human Interface Design, the new material _“combines the optical qualities of glass with a fluidity only Apple can achieve, as it transforms depending on your content or context.”_ The interface elements refract and reflect their surroundings using real-time rendering and specular highlights that respond to device movement.

## visionOS 26 introduces spatial computing advances

visionOS 26 delivers groundbreaking spatial experiences, with **spatial widgets** as the flagship feature. Users can position widgets like clocks, weather displays, and music players in fixed locations within their environment, even embedding them into walls.

Other highlights include:

- **Enhanced Personas** with more realistic representations and full side-profile views.
- **Spatial scenes powered by generative AI**, letting users lean in and look around photos with lifelike depth.
- Support for **180°/360°/wide FOV content** from Insta360, GoPro, and Canon.
- **PlayStation VR2 Sense controller** support for enhanced gaming.

## iOS 26 brings intelligence and performance

iOS 26 arrives with enhanced **Apple Intelligence** features:

- **Live Translation** across Messages, FaceTime, and Phone apps.
- Updates to **Visual Intelligence**, enabling screenshots with searchable and actionable content.
- New **screening tools** for calls and messages.
- A redesigned **Games app**.
- Upgrades across **CarPlay, Apple Music, Maps, and Wallet**.

Supported devices range from **iPhone 11 through iPhone 17**, with iPhone XS, XS Max, and XR excluded.

## Unified release across all platforms

September 15 will also see the simultaneous release of **iPadOS 26, macOS Tahoe 26, watchOS 26, tvOS 26, and visionOS 26**. This marks Apple’s shift to **year-aligned version numbers**, skipping directly from iOS 18 to iOS 26 to match the 2025 release year.

The **iPhone 17 series** will ship with iOS 26 pre-installed when devices become available on **September 19**, with pre-orders beginning **September 12**. Apple released **candidate versions** of all operating systems to developers today, signaling readiness for public launch.
